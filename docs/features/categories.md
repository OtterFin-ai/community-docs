# Categories

Categories classify your transactions into meaningful groups — groceries, rent, salary, and so on. They are the primary way OtterFin organizes spending and income for budgets and reports.

## Category types

Categories are either **income** or **expense** categories. This distinction determines how they appear in reports and budgets:

- Expense categories represent money going out
- Income categories represent money coming in

## Hierarchical categories

Categories support a parent/child hierarchy. You can create broad parent categories (like _Food_) and nest more specific subcategories under them (like _Groceries_, _Restaurants_, _Coffee_). Reports roll up subcategory totals into their parent.

This lets you track detailed spending when you need it, while still getting high-level summaries without extra work.

## Managing categories

Categories are managed in **Settings → Categories** (accessible from the household menu in the sidebar). You can create, rename, and delete categories from there.

Deleting a category that has existing transactions assigned to it will leave those transactions uncategorized — reassign them first if you want to maintain clean reporting.

## Assigning categories to transactions

Categories are assigned when you create or edit a transaction. For split transactions, each split line gets its own category.

## Imported categories

If you migrate from Banktivity, your existing income and expense categories are imported automatically as part of the migration process. See [Migrating from Banktivity](../migrating/banktivity.md) for details.
